# The Solo Founder's Guide to Shipping AI Products

I've shipped multiple AI products as a solo founder. Here's the framework I use to go from idea to production without burning out.

## The Stack That Lets You Move Fast

My stack is intentionally boring: Next.js for everything frontend and backend, Supabase for auth and database, Claude API for AI capabilities, and Vercel for deployment. This stack lets me ship a full product in 2-3 weeks.

## The 72-Hour MVP Rule

If I can't build a working MVP in 72 hours, the scope is too big. I ruthlessly cut features until the core value proposition fits in a weekend sprint. Polish comes later — validation comes first.

## Distribution is Half the Battle

Building the product is just the beginning. I write about what I'm building on LinkedIn, share progress publicly, and get feedback from the CA community. This builds an audience before the product launches.

## The Solo Founder Mindset

You won't have a co-founder to validate your ideas or pick you up when things break at 2 AM. But you also won't have anyone slowing you down. The speed advantage of being solo is real — use it.

Ship fast. Learn faster. And always keep chai within arm's reach.
